The Back of the New $20 Bill

Have you seen the "updated" $20 Notes? The U.S. Treasury has rolled out another revamp of the U.S. twenty dollar bill in an effort to stop those counterfeiters once and for all. It features high-tech fake-busting elements like a watermark, a security thread, and color-shifting ink.

We are not going to concern ourselves with a critique of the front of the bill. It's the back of the note that's unusual.

Too Many 20s!!!

In particular, it's the random placement of all those little 20s in the white space that are of particular concern.

Are they supposed to be another security feature? Did they let a novice at the Bureau of Engraving and Printing design it? No one knows!

Connect-the-Dots

There must be more to it. Perhaps the new 20s contain subliminal connect-the-dots messages, like tiny constellations. Or, maybe the 20s connect to form a secret message. Again, no one knows!

You are all encouraged to get a new $20 bill, connect the dots to find the message on the back, and compare it to the suggestions by other when you visit the (imaginary) site newTwenties.org.
